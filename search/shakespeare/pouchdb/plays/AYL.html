  1. ORLANDO

  14. mightily grace himself on thee, he will practise
  15. against thee by poison, entrap thee by some
  16. treacherous device and never leave thee till he

  19. it, there is not one so young and so villanous this
  20. day living. I speak but brotherly of him; but
  21. should I anatomize him to thee as he is, I must
  22. blush and weep and thou must look pale and wonder.
  1. CHARLES
  2. I am heartily glad I came hither to you. If he come
  3. to-morrow, I'll give him his payment: if ever he go
  4. alone again, I'll never wrestle for prize more: and

  1. OLIVER
  2. Farewell, good Charles.
  3. Exit CHARLES

  5. an end of him; for my soul, yet I know not why,
  6. hates nothing more than he. Yet he's gentle, never
  7. schooled and yet learned, full of noble device, of

  9. in the heart of the world, and especially of my own
  10. people, who best know him, that I am altogether
  11. misprised: but it shall not be so long; this

  13. I kindle the boy thither; which now I'll go about.

  1. DUKE SENIOR
  2. Welcome, young man;
  3. Thou offer'st fairly to thy brothers' wedding:
  4. To one his lands withheld, and to the other
  5. A land itself at large, a potent dukedom.
  6. First, in this forest, let us do those ends
  7. That here were well begun and well begot:
  8. And after, every of this happy number
  9. That have endured shrewd days and nights with us
  10. Shall share the good of our returned fortune,
  11. According to the measure of their states.
  12. Meantime, forget this new-fall'n dignity
  13. And fall into our rustic revelry.
  14. Play, music! And you, brides and bridegrooms all,
  15. With measure heap'd in joy, to the measures fall.
  1. JAQUES
  2. Sir, by your patience. If I heard you rightly,
  3. The duke hath put on a religious life
  4. And thrown into neglect the pompous court?
  1. JAQUES DE BOYS
  2. He hath.
  1. JAQUES
  2. To him will I : out of these convertites
  3. There is much matter to be heard and learn'd.
  4. To DUKE SENIOR
  5. You to your former honour I bequeath;
  6. Your patience and your virtue well deserves it:
  7. To ORLANDO
  8. You to a love that your true faith doth merit:
  9. To OLIVER
  10. You to your land and love and great allies:
  11. To SILVIUS
  12. You to a long and well-deserved bed:
  13. To TOUCHSTONE
  14. And you to wrangling; for thy loving voyage
  15. Is but for two months victuall'd. So, to your pleasures:
  16. I am for other than for dancing measures.
