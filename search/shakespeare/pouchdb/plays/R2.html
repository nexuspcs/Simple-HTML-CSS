ACT I

SCENE I. London. KING RICHARD II's palace.

Enter KING RICHARD II, JOHN OF GAUNT, with other Nobles and Attendants
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. Old John of Gaunt, time-honour'd Lancaster,
  3. Hast thou, according to thy oath and band,
  4. Brought hither Henry Hereford thy bold son,
  5. Here to make good the boisterous late appeal,
  6. Which then our leisure would not let us hear,
  7. Against the Duke of Norfolk, Thomas Mowbray?
  1. JOHN OF GAUNT
  2. I have, my liege.
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. Tell me, moreover, hast thou sounded him,
  3. If he appeal the duke on ancient malice;
  4. Or worthily, as a good subject should,
  5. On some known ground of treachery in him?
  1. JOHN OF GAUNT
  2. As near as I could sift him on that argument,
  3. On some apparent danger seen in him
  4. Aim'd at your highness, no inveterate malice.
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. Then call them to our presence; face to face,
  3. And frowning brow to brow, ourselves will hear
  4. The accuser and the accused freely speak:
  5. High-stomach'd are they both, and full of ire,
  6. In rage deaf as the sea, hasty as fire.

  20. Or any other ground inhabitable,
  21. Where ever Englishman durst set his foot.

  23. By all my hopes, most falsely doth he lie.

  2. Pale trembling coward, there I throw my gage,
  3. Disclaiming here the kindred of the king,
  4. And lay aside my high blood's royalty,
  5. Which fear, not reverence, makes thee to except.
  6. If guilty dread have left thee so much strength
  7. As to take up mine honour's pawn, then stoop:
  8. By that and all the rites of knighthood else,
  9. Will I make good against thee, arm to arm,
  10. What I have spoke, or thou canst worse devise.
  1. THOMAS MOWBRAY
  2. I take it up; and by that sword I swear
  3. Which gently laid my knighthood on my shoulder,
  4. I'll answer thee in any fair degree,
  5. Or chivalrous design of knightly trial:
  6. And when I mount, alive may I not light,
  7. If I be traitor or unjustly fight!
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. What doth our cousin lay to Mowbray's charge?
  3. It must be great that can inherit us
  4. So much as of a thought of ill in him.
  1. HENRY BOLINGBROKE
  2. Look, what I speak, my life shall prove it true;
  3. That Mowbray hath received eight thousand nobles
  4. In name of lendings for your highness' soldiers,
  5. The which he hath detain'd for lewd employments,
  6. Like a false traitor and injurious villain.
  7. Besides I say and will in battle prove,
  8. Or here or elsewhere to the furthest verge

  10. That all the treasons for these eighteen years
  11. Complotted and contrived in this land
  12. Fetch from false Mowbray their first head and spring.

  15. That he did plot the Duke of Gloucester's death,
  16. Suggest his soon-believing adversaries,
  17. And consequently, like a traitor coward,
  18. Sluiced out his innocent soul through streams of blood:
  19. Which blood, like sacrificing Abel's, cries,
  20. Even from the tongueless caverns of the earth,
  21. To me for justice and rough chastisement;
  22. And, by the glorious worth of my descent,
  23. This arm shall do it, or this life be spent.
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. How high a pitch his resolution soars!
  3. Thomas of Norfolk, what say'st thou to this?
  1. THOMAS MOWBRAY
  2. O, let my sovereign turn away his face
  3. And bid his ears a little while be deaf,
  4. Till I have told this slander of his blood,
  5. How God and good men hate so foul a liar.
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. Mowbray, impartial are our eyes and ears:
  3. Were he my brother, nay, my kingdom's heir,
  4. As he is but my father's brother's son,
  5. Now, by my sceptre's awe, I make a vow,
  6. Such neighbour nearness to our sacred blood
  7. Should nothing privilege him, nor partialize
  8. The unstooping firmness of my upright soul:
  9. He is our subject, Mowbray; so art thou:
  10. Free speech and fearless I to thee allow.
  1. THOMAS MOWBRAY
  2. Then, Bolingbroke, as low as to thy heart,
  3. Through the false passage of thy throat, thou liest.
  4. Three parts of that receipt I had for Calais
  5. Disbursed I duly to his highness' soldiers;
  6. The other part reserved I by consent,
  7. For that my sovereign liege was in my debt
  8. Upon remainder of a dear account,
  9. Since last I went to France to fetch his queen:
  10. Now swallow down that lie. For Gloucester's death,
  11. I slew him not; but to my own disgrace
  12. Neglected my sworn duty in that case.
  13. For you, my noble Lord of Lancaster,
  14. The honourable father to my foe
  15. Once did I lay an ambush for your life,
  16. A trespass that doth vex my grieved soul
  17. But ere I last received the sacrament
  18. I did confess it, and exactly begg'd
  19. Your grace's pardon, and I hope I had it.
  20. This is my fault: as for the rest appeall'd,
  21. It issues from the rancour of a villain,
  22. A recreant and most degenerate traitor
  23. Which in myself I boldly will defend;
  24. And interchangeably hurl down my gage
  25. Upon this overweening traitor's foot,
  26. To prove myself a loyal gentleman
  27. Even in the best blood chamber'd in his bosom.
  28. In haste whereof, most heartily I pray
  29. Your highness to assign our trial day.
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. Wrath-kindled gentlemen, be ruled by me;
  3. Let's purge this choler without letting blood:
  4. This we prescribe, though no physician;
  5. Deep malice makes too deep incision;
  6. Forget, forgive; conclude and be agreed;
  7. Our doctors say this is no month to bleed.
  8. Good uncle, let this end where it begun;
  9. We'll calm the Duke of Norfolk, you your son.
  1. JOHN OF GAUNT
  2. To be a make-peace shall become my age:
  3. Throw down, my son, the Duke of Norfolk's gage.
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. And, Norfolk, throw down his.
  1. JOHN OF GAUNT
  2. When, Harry, when?
  3. Obedience bids I should not bid again.
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. Norfolk, throw down, we bid; there is no boot.
  1. THOMAS MOWBRAY
  2. Myself I throw, dread sovereign, at thy foot.
  3. My life thou shalt command, but not my shame:
  4. The one my duty owes; but my fair name,
  5. Despite of death that lives upon my grave,
  6. To dark dishonour's use thou shalt not have.
  7. I am disgraced, impeach'd and baffled here,
  8. Pierced to the soul with slander's venom'd spear,
  9. The which no balm can cure but his heart-blood
  10. Which breathed this poison.
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. Rage must be withstood:
  3. Give me his gage: lions make leopards tame.
  1. THOMAS MOWBRAY
  2. Yea, but not change his spots: take but my shame.
  3. And I resign my gage. My dear dear lord,
  4. The purest treasure mortal times afford
  5. Is spotless reputation: that away,
  6. Men are but gilded loam or painted clay.
  7. A jewel in a ten-times-barr'd-up chest
  8. Is a bold spirit in a loyal breast.
  9. Mine honour is my life; both grow in one:
  10. Take honour from me, and my life is done:
  11. Then, dear my liege, mine honour let me try;
  12. In that I live and for that will I die.
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. Cousin, throw up your gage; do you begin.
  1. HENRY BOLINGBROKE
  2. O, God defend my soul from such deep sin!
  3. Shall I seem crest-fall'n in my father's sight?
  4. Or with pale beggar-fear impeach my height
  5. Before this out-dared dastard? Ere my tongue
  6. Shall wound my honour with such feeble wrong,
  7. Or sound so base a parle, my teeth shall tear
  8. The slavish motive of recanting fear,
  9. And spit it bleeding in his high disgrace,
  10. Where shame doth harbour, even in Mowbray's face.
Exit JOHN OF GAUNT
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. We were not born to sue, but to command;
  3. Which since we cannot do to make you friends,
  4. Be ready, as your lives shall answer it,
  5. At Coventry, upon Saint Lambert's day:
  6. There shall your swords and lances arbitrate
  7. The swelling difference of your settled hate:
  8. Since we can not atone you, we shall see
  9. Justice design the victor's chivalry.
  10. Lord marshal, command our officers at arms
  11. Be ready to direct these home alarms.

  51. I wasted time, and now doth time waste me;
  52. For now hath time made me his numbering clock:
  53. My thoughts are minutes; and with sighs they jar
  54. Their watches on unto mine eyes, the outward watch,
  55. Whereto my finger, like a dial's point,
  56. Is pointing still, in cleansing them from tears.
  57. Now sir, the sound that tells what hour it is
  58. Are clamorous groans, which strike upon my heart,
  59. Which is the bell: so sighs and tears and groans
  60. Show minutes, times, and hours: but my time
  61. Runs posting on in Bolingbroke's proud joy,
  62. While I stand fooling here, his Jack o' the clock.
  63. This music mads me; let it sound no more;
  64. For though it have holp madmen to their wits,
  65. In me it seems it will make wise men mad.
  66. Yet blessing on his heart that gives it me!
  67. For 'tis a sign of love; and love to Richard
  68. Is a strange brooch in this all-hating world.
Enter a Groom of the Stable
  1. Groom
  2. Hail, royal prince!
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. Thanks, noble peer;
  3. The cheapest of us is ten groats too dear.
  4. What art thou? and how comest thou hither,
  5. Where no man never comes but that sad dog
  6. That brings me food to make misfortune live?
  1. Groom
  2. I was a poor groom of thy stable, king,
  3. When thou wert king; who, travelling towards York,
  4. With much ado at length have gotten leave
  5. To look upon my sometimes royal master's face.
  6. O, how it yearn'd my heart when I beheld
  7. In London streets, that coronation-day,
  8. When Bolingbroke rode on roan Barbary,
  9. That horse that thou so often hast bestrid,
  10. That horse that I so carefully have dress'd!
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. Rode he on Barbary? Tell me, gentle friend,
  3. How went he under him?
  1. Groom
  2. So proudly as if he disdain'd the ground.
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. So proud that Bolingbroke was on his back!
  3. That jade hath eat bread from my royal hand;
  4. This hand hath made him proud with clapping him.
  5. Would he not stumble? would he not fall down,
  6. Since pride must have a fall, and break the neck
  7. Of that proud man that did usurp his back?
  8. Forgiveness, horse! why do I rail on thee,
  9. Since thou, created to be awed by man,
  10. Wast born to bear? I was not made a horse;
  11. And yet I bear a burthen like an ass,
  12. Spurr'd, gall'd and tired by jouncing Bolingbroke.
Enter Keeper, with a dish
  1. Keeper
  2. Fellow, give place; here is no longer stay.
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. If thou love me, 'tis time thou wert away.
  1. Groom
  2. What my tongue dares not, that my heart shall say.
Exit
  1. Keeper
  2. My lord, will't please you to fall to?
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. Taste of it first, as thou art wont to do.
  1. Keeper
  2. My lord, I dare not: Sir Pierce of Exton, who
  3. lately came from the king, commands the contrary.
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. The devil take Henry of Lancaster and thee!
  3. Patience is stale, and I am weary of it.
Beats the keeper
  1. Keeper
  2. Help, help, help!
Enter EXTON and Servants, armed
  1. KING RICHARD II
  2. How now! what means death in this rude assault?
  3. Villain, thy own hand yields thy death's instrument.
  4. Snatching an axe from a Servant and killing him
  5. Go thou, and fill another room in hell.
  6. He kills another. Then Exton strikes him down
  7. That hand shall burn in never-quenching fire
  8. That staggers thus my person. Exton, thy fierce hand
  9. Hath with the king's blood stain'd the king's own land.
  10. Mount, mount, my soul! thy seat is up on high;
  11. Whilst my gross flesh sinks downward, here to die.
Dies
  1. EXTON
  2. As full of valour as of royal blood:
  3. Both have I spill'd; O would the deed were good!
  4. For now the devil, that told me I did well,
  5. Says that this deed is chronicled in hell.
  6. This dead king to the living king I'll bear
  7. Take hence the rest, and give them burial here.
Exeunt

SCENE VI. Windsor castle.

Flourish. Enter HENRY BOLINGBROKE, DUKE OF YORK, with other Lords, and Attendants
  1. HENRY BOLINGBROKE
  2. Kind uncle York, the latest news we hear
  3. Is that the rebels have consumed with fire
  4. Our town of Cicester in Gloucestershire;
  5. But whether they be ta'en or slain we hear not.
  6. Enter NORTHUMBERLAND
  7. Welcome, my lord what is the news?
  1. NORTHUMBERLAND
  2. First, to thy sacred state wish I all happiness.
  3. The next news is, I have to London sent
  4. The heads of Oxford, Salisbury, Blunt, and Kent:
  5. The manner of their taking may appear
  6. At large discoursed in this paper here.
  1. HENRY BOLINGBROKE
  2. We thank thee, gentle Percy, for thy pains;
  3. And to thy worth will add right worthy gains.
Enter LORD FITZWATER
  1. LORD FITZWATER
  2. My lord, I have from Oxford sent to London
  3. The heads of Brocas and Sir Bennet Seely,
  4. Two of the dangerous consorted traitors
  5. That sought at Oxford thy dire overthrow.
  1. HENRY BOLINGBROKE
  2. Thy pains, Fitzwater, shall not be forgot;
  3. Right noble is thy merit, well I wot.
Enter HENRY PERCY, and the BISHOP OF CARLISLE
  1. HENRY PERCY
  2. The grand conspirator, Abbot of Westminster,
  3. With clog of conscience and sour melancholy
  4. Hath yielded up his body to the grave;
  5. But here is Carlisle living, to abide
  6. Thy kingly doom and sentence of his pride.
  1. HENRY BOLINGBROKE
  2. Carlisle, this is your doom:
  3. Choose out some secret place, some reverend room,
  4. More than thou hast, and with it joy thy life;
  5. So as thou livest in peace, die free from strife:
  6. For though mine enemy thou hast ever been,
  7. High sparks of honour in thee have I seen.
Enter EXTON, with persons bearing a coffin
  1. EXTON
  2. Great king, within this coffin I present
  3. Thy buried fear: herein all breathless lies
  4. The mightiest of thy greatest enemies,
  5. Richard of Bordeaux, by me hither brought.
  1. HENRY BOLINGBROKE
  2. Exton, I thank thee not; for thou hast wrought
  3. A deed of slander with thy fatal hand
  4. Upon my head and all this famous land.
  1. EXTON
  2. From your own mouth, my lord, did I this deed.
  1. HENRY BOLINGBROKE
  2. They love not poison that do poison need,
  3. Nor do I thee: though I did wish him dead,
  4. I hate the murderer, love him murdered.
  5. The guilt of conscience take thou for thy labour,
  6. But neither my good word nor princely favour:
  7. With Cain go wander through shades of night,
  8. And never show thy head by day nor light.
  9. Lords, I protest, my soul is full of woe,
  10. That blood should sprinkle me to make me grow:
  11. Come, mourn with me for that I do lament,
  12. And put on sullen black incontinent:
  13. I'll make a voyage to the Holy Land,
  14. To wash this blood off from my guilty hand:
  15. March sadly after; grace my mournings here;
  16. In weeping after this untimely bier.
Exeunt
